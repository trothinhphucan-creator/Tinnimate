# TinniMate — Brand Guidelines

**Product:** TinniMate
**Market:** Vietnam + Vietnamese-speaking communities
**Stage:** Pre-launch (Brand identity established for public-facing materials)

## Brand Purpose & Vision

### Mission
Empower Vietnamese adults to reclaim peace and quality of life by providing science-backed, accessible tinnitus therapy combining proprietary technology, clinical rigor, and compassionate support.

### Vision
A world where tinnitus doesn't control life. Where Vietnamese people can sleep peacefully, work with focus, and live free from the burden of constant ringing.

### Brand Promise
"TinniMate is your science-backed companion in tinnitus management. We combine cutting-edge sound technology with clinical depth, Vietnamese language support, and 24/7 AI guidance—so you can regain control."

## Brand Voice & Tone

### Voice Characteristics
**Empathetic:** We understand the emotional weight of tinnitus. We speak to both suffering and hope.
**Informed:** We're grounded in neuroscience and clinical evidence, not pseudo-science.
**Accessible:** We explain complex concepts simply, avoiding medical jargon.
**Action-Oriented:** We empower users to take steps, not sit passively.
**Vietnamese-First:** We honor Vietnamese language and cultural context.

### Tone Variations by Context

#### 1. Marketing/Awareness (Warmth + Hope)
- **Goal:** Build emotional connection. Show understanding.
- **Example:** "Ù tai ảnh hưởng đến cuộc sống của bạn. Chúng tôi hiểu, và chúng tôi có giải pháp."
  (Tinnitus affects your life. We understand, and we have solutions.)
- **Avoid:** Clinical, cold, dismissive. Never minimize suffering.

#### 2. Educational Content (Clarity + Authority)
- **Goal:** Build trust through knowledge. Explain "why."
- **Example:** "Ù tai không phát sinh từ tai của bạn, mà từ não bộ tự tạo ra tín hiệu âm thanh. Đây không phải bệnh, mà là tín hiệu não bộ cần chú ý."
  (Tinnitus isn't generated by your ear, but by your brain creating sound signals. This isn't a disease, but a signal your brain needs attention.)
- **Avoid:** Oversimplification. "It's all in your head" (dismissive). Jargon without explanation.

#### 3. In-App Chat (Conversational + Supportive)
- **Goal:** Feel like talking to a knowledgeable friend, not a chatbot.
- **Example:** "Mình hiểu việc mất ngủ vì ù tai là rất khó khăn. Hôm nay bạn muốn thử cái gì? Nghe âm thanh, bài tập thở, hay cả hai?"
  (I understand sleep loss from tinnitus is really hard. What do you want to try today? Therapy sounds, breathing exercise, or both?)
- **Avoid:** Robotic responses. Overly formal language. Missing the emotional beat.

#### 4. Customer Support (Professional + Human)
- **Goal:** Solve problems quickly. Show genuine care.
- **Example:** "Cảm ơn bạn đã liên hệ. Chúng tôi biết vấn đề này rất nản chí. Hãy thử bước sau..."
  (Thank you for reaching out. We know this issue is frustrating. Try this next step...)
- **Avoid:** Templated responses. Defensive tone. Lack of accountability.

## Visual Brand Identity

### Color Palette

#### Primary Colors
- **Brand Blue (Trust + Calm):** `#4A90E2` or `#0047AB`
  - Used for: CTAs, primary buttons, key UI elements
  - Psychology: Trust, calm, professionalism (medical context)

- **Warm Accent (Hope + Energy):** `#F39C12` or `#FF9800` (warm orange/amber)
  - Used for: Progress indicators, success states, testimonials
  - Psychology: Hope, warmth, approachability

#### Secondary Colors
- **Soft Purple (Meditation + Introspection):** `#9B59B6`
  - Used for: Relaxation features, visualization exercises

- **Healing Green (Nature + Recovery):** `#27AE60`
  - Used for: Sound therapy, nature sounds, wellness

- **Soft Gray (Clarity):** `#34495E` (text), `#ECF0F1` (backgrounds)
  - Used for: Readability, neutral backgrounds

#### Dark Mode Palette
- **Dark background:** `#1A1A1A` or `#0F0F0F`
- **Light text:** `#F5F5F5`
- **Accent (adjusted):** `#5BA3FF` (brighter blue for contrast)

### Typography

#### Font Stack (Web)
**Headings (Bold, Clear):**
- Primary: `Inter` (modern, geometric, Vietnamese-friendly)
- Fallback: `Segoe UI`, `Roboto`, sans-serif
- Weights: 600 (headings), 700 (taglines)

**Body Text (Readable):**
- Primary: `Inter` (consistent, clean)
- Fallback: `Segoe UI`, `Roboto`, sans-serif
- Weights: 400 (body), 500 (emphasis)
- Size: 16px (mobile), 18px (desktop) for accessibility

**Font Sizes (Hierarchy):**
- H1 (Hero tagline): 36–48px
- H2 (Section heading): 24–32px
- H3 (Subheading): 18–24px
- Body: 16–18px
- Small (captions, fine print): 12–14px

### Imagery & Photography

#### Photography Style
- **Authentic people:** Real users, real emotions (not stock models)
- **Warm lighting:** Golden hour, soft lighting (not harsh clinical lighting)
- **Diversity:** Vietnamese faces, ages 25–60, diverse life circumstances
- **Emotion-forward:** Show relief, peace, improvement (not just smiling faces)
- **Context:** People sleeping peacefully, working focused, living normally

#### Imagery Tone
- Calm, meditative, hopeful (not sterile)
- Avoid: Overly clinical, scaremongering, overly happy (unrealistic)
- Include: Diverse socioeconomic backgrounds (resonates with broader Vietnamese market)

#### Illustration Style (Optional)
- Soft, rounded shapes (not angular)
- Pastel colors or brand color palette
- Friendly characters (Tinni mascot option)
- Use for: Onboarding, empty states, explanatory diagrams

### Logo & Wordmark
- **Logo Concept:** Stylized ear + sound wave OR concentric circles (representing sound ripples + habituation)
- **Wordmark:** "TinniMate" in bold, custom `Inter` with rounded corners
- **Tagline (optional):** "Bạn không đơn độc" underneath or standalone
- **Color variations:** Full color (blue + orange), monochrome (for print), inverted (for dark backgrounds)

### Icons & UI Elements
- **Icon Style:** Rounded, friendly (not sharp)
- **Icon Colors:** Mix of brand colors (blue for actions, orange for progress)
- **Buttons:** Rounded corners (16–24px border radius), clear affordance
- **Loading states:** Animated sound waves or pulse (representing audio)

## Content Standards & Do's/Don'ts

### Do's
- ✓ **Use Vietnamese terminology:** "ù tai" not just "tinnitus". "Ngủ yên" not just "sleep well".
- ✓ **Cite evidence:** Link studies. Reference WHO, peer-reviewed research.
- ✓ **Show progress:** Before/after stories, graphs, concrete improvements.
- ✓ **Empower agency:** "You can control this" not "You're stuck with this."
- ✓ **Use "we":** "Chúng tôi hiểu" (We understand) builds rapport.
- ✓ **Be specific:** "51% of users report better sleep in 3 weeks" not "Many users see results."
- ✓ **Address emotions:** Acknowledge anxiety, frustration, isolation. Then provide hope.
- ✓ **Use social proof:** Testimonials, user count, app ratings (4.5+ stars).
- ✓ **Call to action:** Always offer next step (Download, Try free, Book consultation).
- ✓ **Mobile-first:** All content designed for 5-inch screens first.

### Don'ts
- ✗ **Never diagnose:** "You have tinnitus" OK, "Your tinnitus is caused by X" risky (unless user self-reported).
- ✗ **Never prescribe medication:** "Talk to your doctor" OK, "Take this drug" never.
- ✗ **Never minimize:** "It's all in your head" (dismissive). "Just relax" (insulting).
- ✗ **Never promise cure:** "Better than any drug" risky. "You WILL sleep better" unprovable.
- ✗ **Never use clickbait:** "Doctors HATE this one trick" (unfunny for health).
- ✗ **Never be condescending:** Avoid overly simplistic language for adults.
- ✗ **Never miss accessibility:** No unreadable colors, no autoplay, no flashing.
- ✗ **Never ignore consent:** Don't email unsolicited, don't pressure signup.
- ✗ **Never ignore data privacy:** Comply with Vietnamese PDPA + GDPR. Be transparent about data use.
- ✗ **Don't translate directly from English:** Localize. "Good sleep" (English idiom) = "Ngủ ngon" (Vietnamese equivalent).

## Brand Usage in Marketing

### Campaign Messaging Framework

#### Awareness Phase (Weeks 1–2)
**Objective:** Build emotional connection. Normalize tinnitus.
**Tone:** Warm, understanding, hopeful
**Messages:**
- "15% of humans experience tinnitus. That's millions of Vietnamese. You're not alone."
- "Silence used to bring peace. Now it brings ringing. There's a scientific reason—and a solution."
- "TinniMate: Science-backed tinnitus therapy, built for Vietnamese."

**Channel:** Facebook, TikTok (organic + some paid)

#### Consideration Phase (Weeks 3–4)
**Objective:** Show how TinniMate works. Reduce friction.
**Tone:** Clear, educational, motivating
**Messages:**
- "How TinniMate works: Personalized sound + clinical tools + 24/7 AI support."
- "Try free. 5 messages daily. 3 sounds. 1 hearing test. No credit card. See results in 2–3 weeks."
- "Endorsed by Vietnamese audiologists. Backed by decades of TRT research."

**Channel:** YouTube, landing page, email, Facebook ads

#### Conversion Phase (Weeks 5–8)
**Objective:** Drive Premium signup. Leverage social proof.
**Tone:** Confident, action-oriented, testimonial-driven
**Messages:**
- "500+ users sleeping better. 4.5-star rating. Join them."
- "Premium at ₫99K/month. That's less than 2 coffee runs. Change your sleep quality today."
- "Get 7-day free trial. Cancel anytime. Most won't."

**Channel:** All channels (paid + organic)

## Social Media Voice by Platform

### Facebook (Professional + Warm)
- **Tone:** Slightly formal but friendly. Community-oriented.
- **Post Length:** 50–150 characters (short), with visual + comment-bait question
- **Format:** Mix of education (50%), social proof (30%), calls-to-action (20%)
- **Frequency:** 3–5 posts/week
- **Hashtags:** #TinnitusMate #ÙTai #SoundTherapy #MentalHealthVietnam
- **Example:** "Nếu ù tai khiến bạn mất ngủ, bạn không đơn độc. 20% dân số trải qua điều này. Bạn đã thử nghe âm thanh trị liệu chưa? Hãy share ở bình luận 👇"

### TikTok (Casual + Educational)
- **Tone:** Friendly, relatable, sometimes humorous (not mocking suffering).
- **Post Length:** 15–60 seconds. Quick, punchy.
- **Format:** Educational shorts (60%), user testimonials (20%), product demos (20%)
- **Frequency:** 3–5 videos/week
- **Hashtags:** #TinnitusTreatment #ÙTai #SoundTherapy #SleepHelp #MentalWellness
- **Example:** Short video: "POV: You finally have silence again. Here's how 👇" (show sound app in use, quick sleep footage, peaceful face)

### YouTube (Authority + Narrative)
- **Tone:** Informative, warm, narrative-driven.
- **Post Length:** 5–15 minutes (hero content), 3–5 minutes (testimonials).
- **Format:** Educational explainers (70%), testimonials (20%), behind-the-scenes (10%)
- **Frequency:** 1–2 videos/week
- **Thumbnails:** Contrasting colors, emotional faces (peaceful, relieved), text overlay
- **Example:** Title: "Tinnitus Explained: Why You Hear Ringing + How to Fix It" (animated explainer, TinniMate features, testimonial close)

### LinkedIn (B2B + Clinical)
- **Audience:** Audiologists, therapists, health professionals
- **Tone:** Professional, evidence-based, thought leadership
- **Format:** Clinical insights (50%), industry news (30%), case studies (20%)
- **Frequency:** 2–3 posts/week
- **Hashtags:** #Audiology #MentalHealth #DigitalHealth #TinnitusManagement
- **Example:** "New research on TRT + sound therapy. Here's how TinniMate integrates both—and the outcomes..."

## Brand Consistency Checklist

### Before Launching Any Content
- [ ] **Tone Check:** Does it feel warm + empowering (not clinical/cold)?
- [ ] **Language Check:** Vietnamese terminology correct? Localized, not just translated?
- [ ] **Visual Check:** Colors consistent? Images authentic (not stock-photo-y)?
- [ ] **Message Check:** Aligned with one of the core messages above?
- [ ] **CTA Check:** Clear next step? (Download, Try free, Learn more, Book consult)
- [ ] **Mobile Check:** Readable on 5-inch screen? Touch-friendly buttons?
- [ ] **Accessibility Check:** Sufficient color contrast? No flashing? Captions on videos?
- [ ] **Legal Check:** Claims evidence-based? No diagnosis/prescription?
- [ ] **Diversity Check:** Do images represent Vietnamese diversity (age, background, wealth)?

## Brand Evolution & Governance

### What Won't Change
- Mission: Empower Vietnamese with tinnitus
- Core voice: Empathetic, informed, accessible
- Primary color: Brand blue (trust)
- Logo/wordmark: Core identity stable

### What Can Evolve
- Accent colors (seasonal campaigns)
- Typography weights (readability improvements)
- Mascot design (if introduced)
- Campaign themes (quarterly refreshes)
- Terminology (as language evolves, medical understanding updates)

### Approval Process for Brand Assets
- **All marketing copy:** Lead (marketing) + Medical advisor (accuracy check)
- **Visual design:** Lead (marketing) + Design system owner
- **Social media:** Community manager can approve (posts + images within templates)
- **Product updates affecting brand:** Product + Marketing alignment
